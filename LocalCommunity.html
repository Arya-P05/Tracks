What to add on your resume

Passing the ATS

Creating a standout resume requires more than just listing your past jobs and education. It's about showcasing your achievements, skills, and the value you can bring to a new role. Here are essential tips on what to write on your resume and how to present your points effectively:

Start with Action Verbs: Begin each point with a strong action verb to convey your accomplishments clearly and powerfully. Verbs like 'Led', 'Developed', 'Implemented', and 'Increased' set a dynamic tone.

Use the XYZ Method: To format your points effectively, employ the XYZ method - where X is the situation or problem, Y is the action you took, and Z is the outcome. This method helps you create impactful statements that highlight your contributions and results. For example, "Increased (X) sales by 20% (Z) through the implementation of a new direct marketing strategy (Y)."

Ensure No Errors: Proofread your resume multiple times to eliminate spelling or grammatical errors. These mistakes can detract from your professionalism and attention to detail.

Use Correct Tense: Write about your current job in the present tense and past roles in the past tense to maintain clarity and consistency throughout your resume.

Incorporate Keywords from the Job Description: Tailor your resume for each application by using keywords and phrases from the job description. This not only demonstrates that you're a good fit but also helps your resume pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

Visit JobScan: For more insights on optimizing your resume for ATS and matching job descriptions, visit JobScan. This tool can analyze how well your resume matches a job posting and suggest improvements.

Remember, a well-crafted resume is your ticket to landing an interview. By starting each point with an action verb, structuring your achievements with the XYZ method, ensuring accuracy, and tailoring your content to the job description, you'll create a resume that stands out to employers.
