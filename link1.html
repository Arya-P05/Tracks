How to Format your resume

Things to remember

When formatting your resume, it's crucial to strike a balance between readability, professionalism, and highlighting your most relevant experiences and skills. Here are key points to remember:

Choose the Right Font: Use a professional, easy-to-read font like Calibri, Arial, or Times New Roman. Keep the font size between 10 and 12 points for the body text and slightly larger for section headings.

Utilize Margins and Spacing: Use standard margins (1” on all sides is a safe bet) to ensure your resume looks tidy and is easy to read. Apply consistent spacing between sections and bullet points to improve readability.

Employ a Logical Structure: Organize your resume in a logical order, typically starting with your name and contact information, followed by a summary or objective, work experience, education, skills, and any other relevant sections (e.g., certifications, volunteer work).

Highlight Key Sections: Use bold and italics sparingly to highlight important information like job titles, employers, or degrees. Consider using bullet points to list achievements or responsibilities, making them easier to scan.

Keep It Concise: Aim for a one-page resume if you have less than 10 years of experience. More experienced professionals can consider a two-page resume. Focus on relevance—only include information that is directly related to the job you’re applying for.

Ensure Consistency: Be consistent in how you format dates, titles, and headings. This includes using the same date format throughout and aligning your text consistently.

Use a Header for Contact Information: Place your name, professional email address, and phone number at the top. You may also include a LinkedIn profile link.

Tailor Your Resume: Customize your resume for each job application. Highlight the experience and skills that align with the job description.

Quantify Achievements: Where possible, use numbers to quantify your achievements. Metrics can provide context and demonstrate the impact of your work.

Avoid Common Mistakes: Steer clear of using first-person pronouns (I, me, my). Double-check for spelling and grammatical errors. Do not include personal information such as age, marital status, or photos unless specifically required.

Use Section Headings: Clearly label each section (e.g., Work Experience, Education) to guide the reader’s eye through your resume.

Optimize for ATS: Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes. Use keywords from the job description and a straightforward layout to ensure your resume is ATS-friendly.

Proofread: Review your resume multiple times and consider having someone else look it over to catch any mistakes you may have missed.

By following these guidelines, you can create a well-organized and professional resume that effectively showcases your qualifications and grabs the attention of hiring managers.
