Classes

A new kind of Sabha, with a holistic curriculum. One that provides practical, interactive, life-based lectures for the development of mukto.

Current State:

In the works of creating a new kind of Sabha, one that is more interactive, relevant and holistic. A classes pilot has been tested out in Toronto.

Challenge:

Develop a classes styled sabha curriculum which empowers the youth to explore Satsang teachings in a meaningful way. This holistic curriculum should:

  • Cater to Youth Needs
  • Include Westernized Context mixed with Satsang Teachings
  • Prioritize Interactive Learning
  • Allow for an Open and Safe Space

Areas of Interest:

Understanding the Youth:What are the specific needs, interests, and learning styles of youth within each youth center?

  • What kind of learning modules and activities can be focused on to ensure interest?

Culturally Relevant Content: How can we adapt Satsang teachings to be culturally relevant for youth living in a Western context.

  • What changes or adaptations can be made to achieve this?

Facilitating Engagement: It is vital to ensure an engaging learning environment. What are ideas for interactive teaching methods, encouraging participation, and creating a safe space for open dialogue?

Maintaining Consistency: A standardized curriculum across all youth centers can help establish clear guidelines for course content and delivery methods. What kind of quality assurance processes can we design?

Project Possibilities:

  • A sample class session/segment
  • Topic List
  • Sample Classes curriculum
  • Questions of the youth (Questions of the youth at the retreat)
  • Feedback and Improvement Processes
  • Digital Toolkits for Engagement
  • Interactive Learning Modules
  • Pipelines to ensure classes are presented consistently across centers
